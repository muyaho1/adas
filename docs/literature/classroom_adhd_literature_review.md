# Literature Review: Classroom ADHD Interventions and Multi-Agent Simulation

This note translates peer-reviewed ADHD classroom intervention papers and
simulation-method papers into concrete design constraints for the classroom
multi-agent simulator.

## Extraction Template

Each paper is summarized with the same fields:

- Research question
- Population / setting
- Intervention or simulation mechanism
- Measurement variables
- Main findings
- How the finding maps to our scenarios / state / reward / transition priors
- Limits to keep in mind

## ADHD Classroom Intervention and Measurement Papers

| Paper | Research question | Population / setting | Intervention / mechanism | Measurement variables | Main findings | Project mapping | Limits |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| DuPaul et al., 2022, *Child Adolesc Psychiatr Clin N Am*, DOI: 10.1016/j.chc.2021.08.003 | Which school interventions have the strongest support for elementary students with ADHD? | Elementary-school students with ADHD | Classroom behavioral strategies, organizational skills training, self-regulation strategies, peer and academic interventions | ADHD symptoms, academic / social / behavioral impairment | Behavioral classroom strategies, organization training, and self-regulation strategies have the strongest support; medication alone is rarely sufficient for school functioning. | Keep the project scope in school transitions and preserve action groups for antecedent supports, reinforcement, and self-regulation scaffolds. Reward should value both compliance and attention. | Review-level summary, not a single transition-specific transition model. |
| Yegencik et al., 2025, *Frontiers in Psychology*, PMID: 40761448 | Do school-based RCTs improve ADHD symptoms and associated school outcomes? | Children / adolescents in school settings | Teacher-, student-, parent-, and multi-component school interventions | Inattention, hyperactivity / impulsivity, academic skills, social skills, externalising behavior | Significant positive effects for inattention, academic outcomes, social skills, and externalising behavior; weaker / non-significant effects for hyperactivity-impulsivity. | Let `attention` and `compliance` improve under effective actions, but avoid unrealistically fast suppression of `escalation_risk` when profile impulsivity is high. | High-level pooled evidence; intervention components and classroom transitions are heterogeneous. |
| Gaastra et al., 2016, *PLOS ONE*, DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0148841 | Do classroom interventions reduce off-task and disruptive behavior in students with ADHD symptoms? | Classroom-based studies of children with ADHD symptoms | Antecedent-based, consequence-based, self-regulation, and combined classroom interventions | Off-task behavior, disruptive behavior | Classroom interventions reduce off-task and disruptive behavior; consequence-based and self-regulation components are especially relevant. | Tie `transition_warning`, `visual_schedule_cue`, `labeled_praise`, `collaborative_problem_solving`, and `sensory_support` to positive `attention/compliance` deltas and lower `distress/escalation` priors. | Effects are averaged across studies and do not provide exact per-turn numeric deltas. |
| Staff et al., 2021, *J Clin Child Adolesc Psychol*, PMCID: PMC8802898 | Which specific behavioral teacher-training techniques reduce ADHD behaviors? | Children with ADHD in classroom teacher-training microtrials | Antecedent-based and consequence-based teacher strategies | Teacher-rated ADHD behavior and classroom functioning | Both antecedent-based and consequence-based techniques can reduce behavioral difficulties; age and classroom context can moderate effects. | Preserve separate action clusters for antecedent supports vs. consequence strategies, and allow profile-dependent response sensitivity. | Not all effects are transition-specific and not every action maps one-to-one to our 12 discrete actions. |
| Staff et al., 2023, *J Atten Disord*, PMCID: PMC10291114 | How does classroom setting relate to observed ADHD behavior? | Children with ADHD and typically developing peers across group lessons, seatwork, and transitions | Naturalistic classroom observation | Off-task behavior, motor hyperactivity, verbal hyperactivity | Children with ADHD are more off-task overall; motor and verbal hyperactivity increase during classroom transitions. | Set transition scenarios with lower starting `attention`, lower `compliance`, and mildly elevated `distress/escalation`. Treat transitions as higher-risk ticks than ordinary instruction. | Observational and not causal; hyperactivity increases during transitions are not always inherently harmful. |
| Staff et al., 2020, *J Atten Disord*, PMCID: PMC8369909 | Are teacher rating scales valid for classroom ADHD assessment? | 22 studies, N = 3,947 | Teacher ratings compared with clinical interviews or structured classroom observations | Inattention, hyperactive-impulsive behavior | Teacher ratings show convergent validity, especially against interviews and hyperactive-impulsive ratings; correlations with observed inattention are lower, so observations add information. | Split “latent student state” from “observer report” in the world model, and keep `attention` noisier / less certain than gross disruption. | Teacher ratings should not be used as a sole ground truth target. |
| Fabiano et al., 2025, *J Consult Clin Psychol*, PMID: 40587316 | Does Daily Report Card intervention reduce rule violations and impairment? | Children receiving school-home behavioral intervention | Daily Report Card and linked behavioral contingencies | Classroom rule violations, ADHD symptoms, impairment | DRC-style feedback can reduce classroom rule violations and improve ADHD-related functioning. | Long-session memory should accumulate `teacher_trust` and `transition_tolerance` when teacher actions are consistently effective and predictable. | Study details need to be checked when full text is available; not every DRC mechanism is transition-specific. |
| Power et al., 2012, *J Consult Clin Psychol*, PMID: 22506793 | Can family-school linked intervention improve school functioning and home-school collaboration? | Children with ADHD receiving family-school intervention | Daily Report Card + consultation / home-school collaboration | Homework, parent-teacher relationship, school-home functioning | Linked family-school intervention improves parent-teacher collaboration and some school-home functioning outcomes. | Even in a school-only v1, preserve a long-horizon memory variable for stable trust / predictability from repeated teacher support. | Home context is outside the v1 simulator scope, so map only the long-horizon reinforcement idea. |
| Korpershoek et al., 2018, PMCID: PMC6179198 | Do behavioral classroom programs improve disruptive behavior and on-task behavior in primary school RCTs? | 19 RCTs, N = 18,074 primary-school children | Behavioral classroom programs | Teacher-rated disruptive behavior, classroom-observed on-task behavior | Programs reduce teacher-rated disruptive behavior and improve observed on-task behavior, but effect sizes are small. | Keep per-turn rewards and transition priors conservative rather than letting one action create huge gains. | Broad classroom programs, not only ADHD diagnosis or transition moments. |
| Matta et al., 2020, *J Sch Psychol*, PMID: 32711722 | Are Direct Behavior Rating trajectories sensitive to intervention-related change? | School-based behavior monitoring studies | Direct Behavior Rating over repeated measurement periods | Academic engagement and disruptive behavior trajectories | Repeated ratings can capture gradual intervention-related behavior change over time. | Add session-level trend curves for `attention`, `distress`, `transition_tolerance`, and `teacher_trust` in evaluation and UI. | DBR is a measurement framework, not a mechanistic transition model. |
| Christ et al., 2014, *Sch Psychol Q*, PMID: 23895317 | How should direct behavior ratings be interpreted across time? | School progress-monitoring context | DBR time-series interpretation and graphing | Trend, level, and change over repeated behavior observations | Time-series visualization supports interpreting gradual behavioral change and intervention response. | Replay UI should show a time axis, not just isolated final outcomes. Session summaries should expose trend statistics. | Does not specify LLM agent architecture. |

## Practical Extraction Rules

- Every scenario or profile prior should cite at least one paper in `evidence`.
- State transition constraints should encode **direction and conservative magnitude**, not exact clinical truth.
- LLM output should provide narrative variation, while numeric state updates are clipped to literature-informed bounds.
- Long-session memory should affect broad trust / tolerance variables slowly, not erase the child profile in a few sessions.
